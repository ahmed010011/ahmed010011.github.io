The most important cities of Algeria:




The shrine of the martyr in the city of Algiers

The shrine of the martyr is one of the most important tourist attractions in Algeria. It is a large monument with a length of 92 m. It is located on the heights of the city of Algiers and can be seen from several different places in the city. This shrine was built in 1982 on the occasion of commemorating the twentieth anniversary of the liberation of Algeria and in memory of the victims of the war of liberation.

The memorial is designed in the form of three palm leaves bearing an eternal flame, and at the beginning of each palm leaf there is a statue of a soldier symbolizing one of the three eras of the war of liberation. Make sure to visit the museum located below the monument which tells a dramatic story about the war when visiting Algeria.

Notre Dame cathedral Africa

The Notre Dame Cathedral of Africa was completed in 1872 AD during French colonial rule, and the architecture of the building is a wonderful mixture of Byzantine and Roman architecture. Designed as a large main dome, blue and white mosaics run around the top of the main body of the church.

The cathedral was built on top of a cliff overlooking the city and the coast. The cathedral is considered a symbol of religious tolerance, so it is one of the most beautiful tourist areas in Algeria that must be visited during tourism in Algeria. This inscription on the church came “Notre Dame d’Afrique priez pour nous et pour les Musulmans” Which means: “Lady of Africa, pray for us and for Muslims.”

Saint Cruz Castle

Saint Cruz Castle and Fort, one of the best tourist areas in Algeria, stands 429 meters above sea level, offering wonderful panoramic views of the city from above. Built by the Spaniards in the second half of the 16th century, this fort is one of three forts connected to each other by tunnels below them.

This fort has a huge rainwater storage tank with an estimated capacity of 300,000 liters. Below the old Spanish fortress is the Santa Cruz Church, which was built by French colonialism in the year 1850 AD. You will not be able to resist this beauty when tourism in Algeria.

Hassan Pasha Mosque

The Hassan Pasha Mosque or the Pasha Mosque, or as it is also called the Great Mosque, is one of the most beautiful tourist attractions in Algeria, which was built by order of Baba Hassan Pasha in 1797 to commemorate the expulsion of the Spaniards from the country. Unfortunately, during the French occupation, the French army seized the mosque and everything in it was looted, but it was returned again to the Algerians to practice their religious rituals there. This mosque was the main mosque for the Muslims of Oran and the place of work of the Mufti.

Plateau Lalla Siti in Tlemcen

She was named so in relation to the righteous and honorable guardian coming from Baghdad, “the daughter of Sheikh Abdul Qadir Al-Jilani,” the author of the famous Sufi order, and she lived between the 6-7 centuries AH, and had dignity and many folk tales were woven around her as the first Sufi woman to domesticate the mountains. And it had a special place in the hearts of the people of the country because of the services it provided to them.

After her death, she was buried in the hill where she was worshiped and which bore her name. The locals celebrate the holy month of Ramadan. The plateau surmounts the plains of Tlemcen and overlooks it as if it were an honest guard protecting the country and the people.

Al-Mashour Palace

The Mechouar Palace is one of the most important landmarks of Tlemcen that must be visited when tourism in Algeria, and is considered a witness to the history of the great city that was always coveted by the colonialists. of the Zayanite kings.

Al-Mashour Palace contains a variety of pavilions built to suit different seasons. The summer pavilion is built of marble to reduce the high temperature, while the winter pavilion is built of stone to keep the place warm.

Sidi M'sid suspension bridge

The suspension bridge of Sidi M'sid, or what is called the suspension bridge, is located at a height of 175 meters above the ground. It is the highest bridges in the city of Constantine and the highest in Africa. The bridge stands tall and overlooks the valley and around it the picturesque mountainous terrain. This suspension bridge connects the city to the hill and remains the most beautiful and most famous bridge in Constantine. If you cross the bridge during windy weather, you will feel a little swaying, but there is no need to be afraid. This is a natural feeling because the bridge is well secured. Do not miss this wonderful adventure when tourism in Algeria.

Prince Abdul Qadir Mosque

The Emir Abdelkader Mosque is one of the architectural masterpieces created by man that you can see when traveling to Algeria. The mosque is one of the largest mosques in North Africa, as it can accommodate 15,000 worshipers at one time.

The mosque, which dates back to the 20th century, has a white facade with high windows, two minarets and a large dome in the middle.

Saint Augustine Church in Annaba

It is a Roman Catholic church erected in the name of St. Augustine, work began in 1880 AD and continued for 20 years, and opened in 1900 AD. The church is an architectural masterpiece that all lovers of tourism in Algeria must see. White marble, red alabaster and hard wood were used in its construction. The church sits on the top of the hill and boasts delightful gardens with a full view of Annaba Bay. The church remains a witness to the history of Christianity in this part of the city. country.

Quraya National Park overlooks the Mediterranean Sea, and is named after the nearby mountain. It includes a wonderful mixture of mountainous terrain with hiking trails, and there are also diverse forests of plants and other organisms, and it has wonderful beaches and ideal for snorkeling, and it has been declared by UNESCO. A world nature reserve in 2004 AD, so we advise you to visit it when you travel to Algeria.

Ghardaia Palace

It is also called the jewel of the oases and is considered the capital of the M’zab Valley. It was built in 1048 AD at the highest point in the M’zab Valley around a rocky plateau. On the top of the plateau is the Great Mosque, which is the central and focal point for the lives of the people there, and the streets rise from the bottom to the top and meet at the mosque. To form a distinct urban fabric.

Surrounding the palace, which you will enjoy seeing when tourism in Algeria, are many tombs that have a special place among the people, and there is also a spacious market square that witnesses an active movement there

Roman ruins in Tipasa

The history of Tipasa goes back thousands of years, and its ancient ruins tell the stories of the Roman civilization that passed through it, as it was a Roman city built on three hills overlooking the sea, with multiple buildings such as palaces and the Roman theater, and the city was surrounded by a great wall and many observation posts that he used The Romans guarded the city, so we advise you to visit it when you visit Algeria.
